Teenagers are going through a transition from a very individualistic way of perceiving the world to a need of binding and position themselves toward their peer.

Collaboration is a skill that should be taught to children since it helps developing empathy and curiosity. It means being able to cooperate, to listen to one another, to debate taking initiatives and having various responsibilities towards each other.

Values are what can bind groups or divide them. They are the core of each debates.
The Empty Island workshop is a tool to teach teens to collaborate by experiencing different roles in the group.

The kids will have to organise their settlement on an Island depending on the values the have to agree on.
